Camping at White Pine National Park

Do you love camping in Minnesota? Enjoy getting away from the hustle and bustle of life? Do you find the sounds and smells of the wilderness relaxing?

Camping in White Pine National Park is an excellent way to appreciate Minnesota's rivers and lakes and relax. The best thing about camping in White Pine is you can really rough it, or you can experience all of the luxuries of what has fondly become known as "glamping." It all depends on the type of experience and amenities you prefer. White Pine National Park has private campgrounds as well as state- and federal-run campgrounds.

Contact us at 218-555-1212 or online to
begin planning your camping vacation today!

Choose Your Style of Camping

The first thing you need to decide is are you interested in “remote” or “park” camping. Remote camping tends to require a certain knowledge of the survival needs being in the backwoods whereas, traditional camping at a park requires other amenities and camping essentials that you should make sure to have on hand.

At White Pine National Park, you can have a fulfilling experience, see the dark, starry skies and watch the sun rise on a quiet, misty morning. We offer rustic cabins, Yurts, tent and camper sites.

Rustic Cabins

Our cabins are nestled into the beautiful landscape of Northern Minnesota surrounded by the sounds of loons, harps of crickets and the rustling wind through the trees. Most of our cabins have hardwood floors. All have full cooking facilities with propane stove/oven, refrigerator, toaster, coffee maker and ample dishes and cookware. Bathrooms include a shower, toilet and sink. There is electricity in most of our cabins, propane heat and the cabins are furnished simply but comfortably. You won’t be bothered by a telephone or TV and there is no wifi. Towels, sheets, and blankets are provided, along with hand soap, dish soap and toilet paper. See all of our cabins and their availability here.

RV's

The White Pine National Park area offers various drive-up campsites at state/federal camp sites. You can use tents, RVs, campers, etc. The parks vary in amenities such as bathrooms, electric and sewer hookups and more! click here to find available sites and amenities for White Pine National Park campground.

Yurt Camping

Yurts are insulated canvas tents with wood floors and woodstoves, available year-round in White Pine National Park. They're perfect for those who want the experience of camping, but don’t want to sleep on the ground!

In addition to functioning windows, each yurt has a wood burning stove, a roof with a skylight, bunkbeds, and a dinner table and chairs. They range from 15 to 23 feet in diameter and sleep 3 to 8 people. Some are wheelchair accessible. click here for varieties, more details and availability.

Tent Camping

Remote and Park experiences available: Enjoy nature in the backwoods along White Pine's various lakes and rivers via canoe, boat, kayak or backpacking. The Details

A deposit is required for each reservation of half the total amount. The following can be booked through whitepinereservations.park or by calling 218-555-1212. Please see below for acceptable forms of payment.

  • Reservations:

    Individual Campsites: May be reserved up to 6 Months in advance.

    Cabins & Yurts: Up to 6 Months in advance

    Group Facilities: Up to 12 months (rolling window) in advance

  • Check-in: after 3pm

    Check-out: 1pm

  • Accessibility:

    Except for Remote Tent Camping, and in some instances Park Tent Camping there is the option of wheelchair accessibility, designed to meet ADA standards. Please see information pertaining to specific lodging for further details.

  • Pets:

    Pets are not allowed inside cabins, yurts or other buildings, lodging facilities, or beach areas.

  • Smoking:

    All cabins, yurts and other buildings are smoke free.

  • Amenities vary by individual location. Please see specific lodge descriptions for further details.
  • Electricity:

    There are electrical hookups for most RV campsites and most cabins.

    All yurts have a wood stove for heat and do not have electricity.

    Please see information pertaining to specific lodging for further details.

  • Firewood:

    In the winter, wood will be provided for the woodstove for yurts only. Wood may be purchased for the Rustic Cabins at the main lodge.

    Please help prevent the spread of tree-killing insects by burning only approved firewood, available at all Minnesota State Parks

  • Methods of payment:

    We Accept all four major credit cards: Visa, Mastercard, American Express and the Discover Card. You can use any of these cards through our online shoppingcart any time day or night, or you can place your order by phone, email.

  • Late Cancellations:

    Late cancellations are those cancelled after 12:00 midnight (CST) two days before arrival.

    For all lodging options, If a party cancels a reservation the day before or on the day of arrival they will be charged a $25.00 service fee.

    If a party cancels a reservation within 14 days of the scheduled arrival date, no service fee will be charged.

  • No-shows:

    Reserved campsites, cabins, yurts and group overnight facilities will be held until check-out time on the day following your scheduled arrival. No-shows, early departures, or cancellations after the scheduled arrival date are not entitled to a refund.

  • Reservation Fee:

    For some facilities an additional reservation fee is charged.
